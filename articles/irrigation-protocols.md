# Irrigation protocols

Shaping removes bulk dentine and creates space. **Irrigation is what actually disinfects.** The instruments cannot reach the isthmuses, fins, lateral canals, and apical deltas where bacteria persist — the irrigant has to.

## Goals

1. **Dissolve organic tissue** (pulp, biofilm).
2. **Disinfect** the canal system.
3. **Remove the smear layer** created by instrumentation.
4. **Flush out debris** continuously through the procedure.

No single solution does all four. Protocols combine solutions.

## Core solutions

### Sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl)

**The primary irrigant.** Dissolves organic tissue and kills essentially all bacteria and fungi relevant to endodontics on contact.

- **Concentration:** 1%–5.25%. Higher concentrations dissolve tissue faster but are more cytotoxic if extruded past the apex.
- **Contact time matters more than concentration.** A 1% solution with 30 min of total contact outperforms a 5.25% solution with 5 min.
- **Warm hypochlorite** (~50°C) is more effective than cold for tissue dissolution.
- **Risks:** extrusion past the apex causes severe pain, swelling, and tissue necrosis ("hypochlorite accident"). Always deliver through a side-vented needle, 2 mm short of the working length, with no binding in the canal.

### EDTA 17%

**Smear-layer remover.** Chelates calcium, demineralizing the smear layer produced by instrumentation so subsequent irrigants can penetrate dentinal tubules.

- Used as a **final rinse** (1 minute), not continuously.
- Do not mix with hypochlorite — they neutralize each other. Flush with saline or water between.

### Chlorhexidine 2%

- Broad-spectrum antimicrobial, including *E. faecalis*.
- **Substantivity** — residual antimicrobial activity for weeks after application.
- Does **not** dissolve tissue. Cannot replace hypochlorite.
- Useful as a final antimicrobial rinse in retreatment or infected cases. Again, do not mix with hypochlorite — forms a brown precipitate (parachloroaniline) that stains and potentially compromises bond strength.

## A reasonable default protocol

| Step | Solution | Duration | Method |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Throughout shaping | 3% NaOCl | Continuous, refreshed every 1–2 min | Side-vented needle, 2 mm short |
| Saline / water flush | Saline | 10 s | Side-vented needle |
| Smear layer removal | 17% EDTA | 1 min | Passive, agitate at 30 s |
| Saline / water flush | Saline | 10 s | Side-vented needle |
| Final antimicrobial | 3% NaOCl | 3 min with agitation | Ultrasonic or multisonic |
| (Optional for infected cases) | 2% CHX | 1 min | After final saline flush |

Total irrigation time: aim for **≥30 minutes of NaOCl contact** across the case for infected teeth; ≥10 minutes for vital pulp cases.

## Agitation

Static irrigant reaches very little of the canal wall beyond the main lumen. **Agitation roughly doubles the antimicrobial effect** of the same volume of hypochlorite.

Options:

- **Manual dynamic agitation** — master gutta-percha cone moved up-and-down in the canal. Free, modestly effective.
- **Sonic** (e.g., EndoActivator) — polymer tip, non-cutting, gentle. Effective and safe.
- **Passive ultrasonic irrigation (PUI)** — small ultrasonic file, non-cutting action, delivers energy that drives streaming and acoustic microstreaming. The most-studied agitation method.
- **Multisonic** (e.g., GentleWave) — pressurized, degassed solution with multi-frequency acoustic energy. Best-in-class cleaning in laboratory studies; high equipment cost.
- **Negative-pressure systems** (e.g., EndoVac) — aspirate from the apex to drive irrigant apically. Reduces extrusion risk, works well in open apices.

## Common mistakes

- **Needle binding in the canal.** Pre-check that the needle moves freely before expressing solution. A bound needle + manual pressure = extrusion.
- **Delivering too close to the apex.** Target 2 mm short of WL with side-vented needles.
- **Skipping the saline flush** between hypochlorite and EDTA or CHX.
- **Under-flushing.** Shaping takes 10–20 minutes of clinical time; irrigation should take about the same, not 30 seconds between files.
- **Cold NaOCl on a complex molar.** Warm it.
