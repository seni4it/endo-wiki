*Disclosure: I paid retail. No relationship with Dentsply Sirona.*

## What I was using before

A corded X-Smart Plus, ~8 years old. Still works, still accurate. The IQ is a step-change in ergonomics but not in clinical outcomes — my shapes were fine on the old motor.

## In use

The cordless handpiece is the feature that justifies the rest. If you've been fighting a cable wrapped around the chair arm for years, this is a tangible daily improvement. Balance is slightly head-heavy vs. the corded unit, but after a week you don't notice.

The iPad app is the other major feature. Large, well-designed display of torque bar, speed, and mode. Presets for Dentsply file sequences (ProTaper Gold, TruNatomy, WaveOne Gold) mean you pick "ProTaper Gold → SX" and the torque and RPM are set. You can customize per file and save.

Apical reverse works correctly when paired with a supported apex locator over the iPad. I use it as a safety, not as a primary working-length method — the reading is still best confirmed on the apex locator directly.

## Reliability

Handpiece and motor: flawless. App: two issues in 10 months. Once the app lost pairing mid-case and I had to reconnect (90 seconds of awkward). Once the iPad woke up from sleep and the app had closed, requiring relaunch. Both are app/OS issues, not motor issues, but they happen in a sterile field and they are annoying.

## Value

At ~$1,800 plus the iPad (another $400–600 for a clinic-dedicated one), total cost of ownership pushes $2,500. Compare to:

- A corded X-Smart Plus at ~$800 new
- Other cordless motors at $1,200–1,500 without the app ecosystem
- Higher-end units like EndoPilot at $4,000+

The IQ is mid-to-high priced. Value depends on how much time you spend on endodontics. For a 2-per-week general dentist, overkill. For a specialist or a generalist doing 10+ RCTs a week, the ergonomics add up over years.

## Verdict

**Recommended if you do a lot of endodontics and value ergonomics.** Not recommended if you already have a working corded motor and fewer than 5 RCTs a week — spend the money on a microscope upgrade or a better irrigation system instead.
