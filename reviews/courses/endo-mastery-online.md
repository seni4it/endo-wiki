## Who this is for

Dentists who need CE credits on a budget and can't travel, or specialists who want a low-cost refresh on a specific topic. Decent for theory. Not a replacement for structured mentorship.

## What it covered

Modular: you buy access to the library and pick which modules to watch. Modules I worked through:

- Diagnosis and treatment planning
- Access cavity design
- Shaping protocols (with a ProTaper Gold emphasis)
- Irrigation and disinfection
- Obturation techniques
- Postoperative pain management

Each module is 3–5 hours of video, ends with a quiz that unlocks the CE certificate.

## What worked

The microscope footage on the shaping and obturation modules is genuinely good — better than I've seen in most online CE. Small details (how a file engages in an S-curve, how gutta-percha adapts during downpack) are visible in a way that's hard to see in-person in a crowded demo.

Self-pace is real. You can watch 20 minutes during lunch and not lose the thread. Downloadable PDF notes mean you can review without rewatching.

The per-hour cost is low — $495 for ~25 hours of video is roughly $20/hour, which compares favorably to live courses.

## What didn't

Some lectures are clearly sponsored. The ProTaper module in particular reads as a Dentsply product tour. The content is still valid but the angle is transparent once you notice it. I'd have preferred a system-neutral treatment of shaping principles with product-specific modules clearly labeled.

The lack of hands-on is the big limitation. You can learn **what** to do from video, but not **how** your hands should feel when you're doing it. For technique acquisition, this is a problem.

Live Q&A could be great but the scheduling is rough if you're not in North America.

## Verdict

**Mixed.** Buy it if you need cheap CE credits and can't travel. Do not buy it expecting to come out of it meaningfully better at the craft — for that, spend the money on a hands-on course instead. As a complement to a hands-on course, this is actually useful: watch the relevant module the night before you try a new technique.
