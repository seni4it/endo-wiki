*Disclosure: I paid the standard course fee myself. No relationship with the organizers.*

## Who this is for

General dentists who already do endodontics regularly and want to move up a level — especially on molars and difficult anatomy. Not a beginner course; you should already be comfortable with rubber dam, rotary/reciprocating files, and working length determination before attending.

## What it covered

Day-by-day, roughly:

1. **Diagnosis and case selection** — when to treat, when to refer, when to consider extraction and implant
2. **Access cavity and pre-flaring** — conservative access, office microscope ergonomics, ultrasonic tips
3. **Shaping** — hands-on with various NiTi systems on extracted molars, including severely curved and S-curved canals
4. **Irrigation and disinfection** — sonic, ultrasonic, and multisonic systems compared live
5. **Obturation and restoration** — warm vertical compaction, carrier-based, immediate post-and-core workflow

## What worked

The faculty's willingness to **show their own failures** was the single most valuable thing. Nobody needs another course where everything goes perfectly on the live treatment demo. Castellucci in particular spent a full morning going through cases that didn't heal, explaining why, and showing the retreatment. That's the kind of thing you cannot learn from a textbook.

The hands-on anatomy bank was real. Some courses use pre-prepared plastic blocks that behave nothing like a molar. These were extracted teeth — mostly human, a few porcine for volume — with calcified chambers, separated files, open apices, and one with internal resorption. You actually learn.

Small groups meant you could screw up on-camera under a microscope and get real feedback, which rarely happens in a 60-person lecture-and-demo format.

## What didn't

Price is a barrier. €3,200 + travel + lodging + lost practice income is easily a €5,000+ week.

Also: if you already use a reciprocating system (WaveOne, Reciproc) as your primary workflow, you'll spend most of your hands-on time on an unfamiliar rotary system, which is partly the point but also frustrating on day 2.

## Verdict

**Yes, I would recommend it and I would do it again.** If your budget allows one big course this year and you already do endo daily, make it this one. If you're a generalist doing 2–3 RCTs a month, a cheaper course with a less intense schedule gives better value for money.
