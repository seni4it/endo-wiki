Editorial guidelines

Short version: be practical, be honest, cite your sources, and respect patient privacy.

For clinical articles

  • Write for a peer. Assume the reader is a dentist or endodontist. Don't explain basic terms unless the whole article is glossary-style.
  • Prefer evidence over opinion. If you state a technique works, cite a study. If it's your personal preference, say so.
  • Link liberally. If you mention another topic that has its own EndoWiki article, link to it.
  • No patient photos or radiographs with identifying information. Crop, redact, or skip.

A good article structure

  1. Lead paragraph — what the topic is, why it matters, in 2–3 sentences.
  2. Indications / background
  3. Technique / content — broken into clear subsections
  4. Outcomes / evidence
  5. See also — links to related EndoWiki articles
  6. References — citations

For reviews

Honesty is the whole point. Readers can get marketing copy from any manufacturer website — EndoWiki is the place for what it's actually like to use the thing.

  • Disclose conflicts. If the manufacturer gave you the course for free, if you're a paid KOL, if you teach for the company — say so at the top of the review.
  • Rate fairly. Use the middle of the scale when it applies. 5-star inflation erodes trust in the whole site.
  • Cover both pros and cons. A 5-star review with zero cons is suspicious.
  • Include price. Price-to-value is half the decision for buyers.

Rating scale

StarsMeaning
★★★★★Would enthusiastically recommend. Essentially no significant complaints.
★★★★☆Recommend with minor caveats.
★★★☆☆Mixed — some will love it, some won't. Depends on use case.
★★☆☆☆Would not recommend for most situations.
★☆☆☆☆Actively regret. Avoid.

Tone

  • Neutral point of view for articles. Present evidence, note disagreements, don't pick sides unless the evidence clearly does.
  • First-person is fine in reviews, not in articles. Reviews are personal experience. Articles are collective knowledge.
  • No promotional language. Avoid "revolutionary", "game-changing", "must-have" unless you're quoting someone else.
  • Don't bash. Criticize the thing, not the company — with specifics, not vibes.

When in doubt

Start a discussion on a similar article. People are friendly.
