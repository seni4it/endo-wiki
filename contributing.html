How to contribute

You don't need to know markdown, HTML, or git. If you can use a word processor, you can contribute to EndoWiki.

The short version

  1. Click the Write a new entry button (top right, or the floating button bottom right).
  2. If it's your first time, click Sign up — email + password, takes 30 seconds. You'll get a confirmation email; click the link, done.
  3. Fill in the form. The body editor works like Google Docs: bold, headings, lists, images in the toolbar. No raw formatting.
  4. Click Publish. Your entry is live on the wiki within a few seconds.

Three kinds of entries

  • Clinical article — a technique, material review, or diagnostic topic written for peers. Aim for practical, evidence-anchored, citable.
  • Course review — your honest take on a CE course, hands-on workshop, or online masterclass you attended. Star rating, pros and cons, price, would-recommend.
  • Equipment review — your experience with an apex locator, endomotor, microscope, file system, or other piece of kit. Same structured format.

What makes a good entry

  • Written for a peer. Assume the reader is another endodontist or dentist with endodontic focus.
  • Evidence over opinion. When you state something works, link to a study when you can.
  • Specific and honest. "The focal length took getting used to" is useful. "Great microscope" is not.
  • No patient-identifying information. Crop out names from radiographs before uploading.

Editing someone else's entry

By default, only the original author can edit their own entry. If you spot an error or want to add a section, use the Start a discussion link on the entry — this opens a thread where you and the author can work it out. (Collaborative editing of single articles is on the roadmap.)

Reviews and discussion

Entries are not pre-moderated — your publish goes live immediately. This is how Wikipedia works too, and it works because everyone is watching. If you see content that doesn't belong, use the discussion link to flag it, or contact a maintainer.

License

All content is released under CC BY-SA 4.0 — you keep authorship, others can share and adapt your work as long as they credit you and release derivatives under the same terms. Perfect for an encyclopedic knowledge base.
